First Principle · v9.6 Canon

The Cosmic Mirror Principle

The foundational doctrine. The spatial axiom that founded existence — and the reason Earth's myths, dreams, and religions feel true even when the world insists they aren't.

Anciuxor encircles all. Aenor sits at the centre. Four stagnant quadrant-zones lie between — Alpha, Dichotomy, Trinity, Omega — and through them every planet orbits. The Aethryx Expanse expresses this cycle in its fullest form. Every other galaxy, in its own way, expresses it too.

The Milky Way and Earth are a lower-activation mirror of the Aethryx Expanse, founded by the Eternal Accord of the Six Cosmic Guardians. Earth is the dormant sleeping-mirror — a world where the cosmos is real only at the edges. What Earth experiences as myth, dream, religion, and fantasy physically exists as reality in the Aethryx Expanse. Human imagination is not invention. It is resonance. It is cosmic memory.

What appears mythic in the rest of the galaxy
was first lived inside someone like you.

The Cosmic Mirror Principle is the saga's spatial axiom — the architecture of existence frozen across worlds. Its developmental counterpart, the Aenoric Trivergence, is the saga's axiom of time: how a creation cycle grows from Genesis through Omen into Definitive — G · O · D, the three-system bloom. Together, the Twin Cosmic Axioms describe the totality of how reality unfolds.

i.
Mythological-Echo
Sub-Principle

Earth's myths, religions, and dream-logic are distorted echoes of true Expanse-mortal history. The Greek gods, Tesla's 3-6-9, scripture, fairy tale — each remembers a fragment of cosmic-tier reality that actually occurred in the higher-activation Expanse. The further humanity reaches with free will, the more clearly the echo rings.

ii.
Periodic-Mirror
Sub-Principle · v6.8 Lock

The mirror operates not only by image but by interval. Cosmic dust seeds the lower-activation worlds at periodic cadences; the Aenoric Convergence (every 7—11 years the planets align in a + orbital spire) is the local heartbeat of the same phenomenon. Pattern reaches outward in beats, not floods.

The Four Realms

Cosmic activation is tiered. The Highest One radiates from total transcendence; each realm beneath holds less of His direct presence and more of created reality. Earth lives at the bottom of the ladder — and only just begins to peer upward.

IV
Omnithris
100% · Backrooms of God

The Highest One alone. No Immortal, Aetheon, Dracolord, or mortal permanently resides; even Anciuxor cannot stay. The source of ultimate truth, judgment, and reality.

III
Anciara
75% · Olympus · Valhalla · Divine Parliament

Anciuxor and the Immortal Council. The pantheon-tier realm — analog to every divine assembly Earth has imagined, all combined. The original tongue (Anciaric) is spoken here.

II
Immortalands
50% · Virtua + Infernia · Echoes Lore

Heaven, hell, nirvana, afterlife — collapsed into one tier. Reincarnation is earnable through Echoes Lore. Viridia cracked open to this realm at Darkenfell (0 CE); Earth has yet to reach it.

I
Universe
≤25% · Aenor 15% · Sun 5% · You Are Here

The physical cosmos. The Aethryx Expanse and the Solar System sit on opposite sides of the same galaxy, undiscovered to one another. Realms deeper than I are earned by accumulated reality-weight — existential events that crack a young world open.

Master Cosmological Doctrine · v5.10

The Aenoric Trivergence

If the Cosmic Mirror is the saga's axiom of space, the Aenoric Trivergence is its axiom of time. Every creation cycle moves through three stages — and a Fourth that the codex leaves open by design.

The Aethryx Expanse expresses creation in three systems — G · O · D. Genesis cradled biology and the first cosmic-tier races. Omen tested devotion, war, and corruption. Definitive refined every principle into balance — and holds Ovauron, the drift world that wanders outside the ordered ring. Earth is the saga's honorable mention: the dormant lower-activation mirror of the Expanse, kept out of the catalog by design.

I · Germination
Genesis
Planets 1—9 · 12 → 9 Bya · cradle of biology + the first cosmic races
II · Omination
Omen
Planets 10—18 · 8.5 → 6 Bya · the testing of devotion · war · corruption
III · Definition
Definitive
Planets 19—27 + Ovauron (28, drift) · 5.8 → 4.85 Bya · refinement · balance · Viridia anchors

Honorable mention — Earth. Not a planet of the Expanse. A lower-activation mirror that begins to remember itself only at the very end of the saga, when Aurellyn arrives.

The Three Wars of Viridia

The Three Wars

Three wars on one world. Three names history could not stop repeating. The Blood War. The Immortal War. The Equinox War. Mortal at first, cosmic by the end — the same shape scaled larger each time.

I.

The Blood War

The First War of Viridia Audrelius's War The Founding
Book IV · Viridia · 333 — 346 CE · 14 Years

"Five regions, one wound — and one name strong enough to bind them."

The first named war on Viridia. Fragmented powers, daemonline corruption, Sylvaerys's rising shadow. Audrelius Veridae X — the tenth of the Aur- line — embarks from the Central region with Amira Silverstone and the bloodline-founders of the age. Battle by battle, region by region, he stitches the world into a single nation. The Blood War ends at the Battle of Civic Square. The Order of the People is founded; Audrelius wears the first true crown of unified Viridia.

Anchor
Audrelius Veridae X · Amira Silverstone · the Central Twelve
Stake
Survival of the five regions · containment of early daemonline spread · whether Viridia would ever be one nation
Outcome
Audrelius unites the regions. The Order of the People is founded — the governance that will hold Viridia for 575 years. The Aur- line becomes the canonical bloodline of Viridian unity.
II.

The Immortal War

The Second War of Viridia Amyra's War Veilfall (1st)
Book VI · Viridia · 908 — 1000 CE

"Kings fought for crowns. Immortals fought for existence itself."

The first open battle of the Virtue Immortals (V+) against the Vice Immortals (V−) on Viridian soil. Emperor Audrelius and King Amyros are assassinated. Queen Empress Amyra Silverstone — daughter of Amyros, last anchor of the Aur- line in this age — inherits a fracturing world. The Order of the People struggles to contain Middle-Realm Infernia outbreaks as the Veil tears for the first time. At the war's climax, Odion — Hatred, Tier A — moves to destroy Mira, Wonder, Tier C. Imperion stands against Odion. Cooperative-opposition, cross-tier defiance. The war's resolution turns on a Vice protecting a Virtue.

Anchor
Queen Empress Amyra Silverstone · the Virtue Immortals · Imperion
Stake
Containment of Infernia leakage · the Aur- bloodline · the survival of the Order of the People itself
Outcome
No clean resolution. The Veil holds, severely weakened. The Order of the People falls; the OVV inherits at 1000 CE. The Aur- line goes dark after Amyra — silence for one thousand years.
III.

The Equinox War

The Third War of Viridia Andre's War Veilfall (2nd)
Book VIII · Viridia · 2026 — 2031 CE

"When gemfire met astral steel, silence followed."

One thousand years of bloodline silence ends. Andre Hart — a bookshop kid in Central, the lost heir — unlocks the Book of Viridia and discovers his line. Khronis surfaces from the shadows where he has manipulated imbalance for nearly two millennia; Khronicore takes the Throne of Darkness; Veilfall the second tears Viridia open. Andre rises against Khronis, becomes Auraxion, and seals him back into the Book. At the war's end, the OVV gives way to the Equinox System — balance between order and chaos, the saga's final mortal governance. The path to the greater cosmos opens through the closing scene.

Anchor
Andre Hart · Auraxion · President Lezlie Stormvale · Valstorm · The Mech
Stake
Survival of the Middle Realm · the Aur- bloodline's return · whether Viridia survives into the cosmic era
Outcome
Auraxion seals Khronis. Equinox replaces the OVV. The Aetherstride is built and launched. Bk IX opens with the path to the Aethryx Expanse already laid.
