### Worldsense

[Heroic Pseudoimmortal — Tier VI] Solion Highreach, formerly an Apex paladin of the Sun-Sworn order, ascended after the Reach of Veil — a battle in which he held a collapsing gate alone for one full sunrise so the refugees of Aurellia could pass through. The act drew the attention of the higher halos, and the morning star itself crowned him. His horse, a Sun-Sworn steed gifted by the elders, was reforged in the same light. He rides at dawn and at decree, and he answers only to the celestial court. Where his blade falls, the wicked lose the right to plan their next move.

> The egg hatches in his hand for one instant. A dragon-shape of nothing falls on the foe and on him both — the foe loses parts of themselves the egg accepts as offerings, and Kravos pays whatever it asks before it leaves.

### Worldsense

[Corrupted Pseudoimmortal — Tier VI] Kravos Dragonsong, once Apex among the Drake-Singers — a lineage that bargained with dragons in song rather than steel — broke the line when he attempted the Shadow Dragon Egg ritual, a rite the elders had sealed for nine generations. The ritual succeeded too well: the egg crowned him with a Pseudoimmortal mantle of Unknown-Void and bound a fragment of dragonsoul to his blood — but it did not stay in the egg. It moved into him. Now the egg is half-hatched at all times, and so is he. His chains drag through the air with their own intentions; his voice carries chords that no longer rhyme. When he fires the egg's full power, the void takes its tithe from him too — but when it lands cleanly, what it consumes does not come back.

**Read:** Solion remains the canonical counter. Kravos's path to victory is **landing 3 clean A2/A3s before Solion lands a single A3** — possible but requires luck on backfire AND drawing 4–5 stack turns. The fight is asymmetric on purpose — a heroic-narrative duel.
